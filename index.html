Engineering Projects

Project 01

Design & Development of Air-Blast Atomizer

Investigated shear-driven liquid breakup in air-blast atomizers to optimize spray atomization for clean combustion systems. Built a custom rig with a Photron high-speed camera, fiber optic illumination, Fluke airflow meter, and Digiten flowmeter. Targeted fuel efficiency improvement and emission reduction for aerospace and energy sectors.

Project 02

Abrasive Water Jet Cutting Machine

Designed and fabricated a low-pressure abrasive water jet cutting machine with 2-axis CNC control. Modeled in Creo; validated nozzle hydraulics in ANSYS Fluent (up to 4.8 x 10⁷ Pa) and structural integrity in ANSYS Mechanical. System included a plunger pump, Arduino microcontroller, stepper motors, and a 0.5 mm nozzle.

Creo CADANSYS FluentANSYS StructuralCNC · Arduino
Project 03

V-10 Engine Design & Analysis

Designed a full V-10 engine assembly in Creo and SolidWorks — crankshaft, camshaft, pistons, connecting rods, fuel rail, oil pan, timing pulleys, valves, and fuel injectors. Performed transient thermal and structural FEA in ANSYS to locate high-stress regions and guide material selection. Generated complete sub-assembly drawings and a bill of materials.

SolidWorksCreoThermal FEAStructural FEA
Project 04

Hazardous Chemical Ventilation Analysis

Modeled benzene dispersion and evacuation in an enclosed room using ANSYS Fluent. Room geometry was built in Creo; transient particle-tracking simulations determined chemical residence time and occupant exposure risk. Results guided optimal ventilation outlet placement for effective hazard mitigation.

ANSYS FluentParticle TrackingVentilation CFDCreo
Project 05

Non-Premixed Turbulent Flame Simulation

Simulated non-premixed turbulent combustion in 2-D and 3-D chambers in ANSYS Fluent. Evaluated combustor length, analyzed swirl-induced air-fuel mixing, and tracked particle residence time under reacting and non-reacting conditions. Increased angular velocity and modified chamber dimensions were identified as routes to improved mixing efficiency.

Combustion ModelingTurbulenceANSYS FluentSwirl Dynamics
Project 06

Flow Through Porous Media — Catalytic Converter

Modeled exhaust gas flow, pressure drop, and uniformity through a catalytic converter substrate. Pressure loss scales with mass flow rate; monolith flow distribution depends on diffuser geometry, Reynolds number, brick resistance, and inlet pipe configuration. Results guided substrate porosity selection to maximize kinetic energy utilization.
